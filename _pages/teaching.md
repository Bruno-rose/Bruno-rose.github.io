# My Teaching Journey

1. Early Teaching Experiences:
   - In school, I conducted math competition workshops for younger students. This was where my passion for teaching began, helping me discover the joy of simplifying complex ideas.

2. University Teaching Assistant Roles:
   - While at the university, I worked as a teaching assistant for various courses, such as Classical Physics, Algorithms and Data Structures, and Methodologies and Programming Design. This experience exposed me to university-level teaching and support.

3. Summer Teaching Assistantship:
   - During summers, I had the opportunity to be a teaching assistant for mathematics courses at the University of Chile's summer school. This role allowed me to contribute to students' education during their break.

4. Teaching in Underprivileged Areas:
   - I taught mathematics to children in underprivileged areas of Santiago as part of the University of Santiago's summer school. It was a fulfilling experience, bringing education to those who needed it most.

5. International Teaching Project:
   - In France, I joined Fanfare sans Frontier, teaching music in primary schools near Palaiseau and in Chile, Peru, and Brazil. Our goal was to teach musical concepts and foster cultural exchange, highlighting the power of education in connecting people.

My teaching journey has been a diverse and humbling experience, which has not only impacted the lives of students but also enriched my own understanding of education's significance. I hope my honest account of these experiences can inspire others to embrace their own path in teaching and cultural exchange.
